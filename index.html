About Me

I am an 19 year old programmer from the United Kingdom. It has been 6 years since I first printed 'hello world' in Python during my very first GCSE Computer Science lesson in year 9, and, since then my language portfolio has grown and continues to do so. I am currently focusing on Front-end web development, perfecting my JavaScript, HTML and CSS skills as well as pursuing a bachelor's degree in Software Engineering after completing an A-level in Computer Science.

Fast-forward to March 2020 and the closure of colleges across the United Kingdom, I suddenly found myself with free time which I used over the next year to improve and learn new features. I challenged myself with projects that would test my abilities and help me improve my code. During this time, Front-end web development became my favourite, resulting in my main project - Fill the Gapz, an online, multiplayer cards against humanity clone.

I am currently studying Software Engineering at University as well as taking part in work experience alongside during any holidays or breaks.

My main focus is to constantly learn, improve and ensure any projects I take on are done to a high standard, accessible and intuitive which holds true for any projects for clients in the past as well as future.
